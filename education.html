Senior Citizens

The Vine Centre is committed to ensuring that local people of all ages have access to learning opportunities, whether they want to improve their skills or qualifications to get a job, help their children with their homeworks, or simply keep their minds active in retirement.


TRAINING COURSES

Various training is available at the Vine Centre including ICT. Most of the training depends on funding that is available.

If you are interested in getting some advice on training and employability call into the Vine Centre and speak to Elizabeth. Training programmes start at various times throughout the year depending on funding.


HEALTHY LIVING CLUB

The Healthy Living Club meets every Monday morning at 9.30am - 11.30am. Come along and meet other people. Activities include:- Fitness, Healthy Eating, Craft, and much more.


EMPLOYABILITY

If you are unemployed or you need help filling out application forms or doing a CV we can help you.


HOMEWORK CLUBS

Homework Clubs are available for children from P1 - P7. They run on Tuesdays at the following times:-

P1 to P3 - 2:15 - 3:15pm

P4 to P7 - 3:15 - 4:15pm

Homework Clubs are free.


LITTLE SPARKS

Little Sparks Mums and Tots runs every Wednesday morning from 9:30am - 11:30am. If you look after a toddler come along and meet other people and enjoy a cup of tea while your child enjoys free play, arts and crafts, story telling and singing.


JAFFA

JAFFA (Jesus a Friend for all) runs every Wednesday afternoon from 3:15pm - 4:15pm for all primary school aged children. In JAFFAyou child will do activites such as arts and crafts, story telling, memory verse learning and singing.


For more information contact Elizabeth Anderson on 9035 1020 or email her directly: eanderson@vinecentre.org
